Podalanka Curry

As a Sri Lankan, knowing how to cook is a rite of passage. Just like how different developers bloom at different stages of life, I was a late bloomer when it came to cooking. This was the first curry I ever made, and today I will share this delicious recipe with y'all!

Ingredients

  • Podalanka/Snake Gourd - About one and a half
  • Coconut Milk - 250 ml
  • Turmeric Powder - Three tbsp
  • Ginger - A small piece
  • Chillies - Three to Four Pieces
  • Curry Leaves - One Branch or about 10 to 12
  • Garlic - One to Two cloves
  • Salt - As you see fit
  • Whole Pepper - One tsp
  • Oil - 100 ml

Instructions

  1. Cut the Podalanka/Snake Gourd into slightly thick pieces - about a pencil's width
  2. Wash thoroughly in water and remove all the seeds
  3. Set aside, if seeds haven't been removed well, you can soak it in water to remove them easily
  4. Cut the chillies into thin and small pieces
  5. Add the chillies, pepper, garlic, curry leaves and ginger into a mortar and pestle and grind thoroughly
  6. Keep the mixture aside and bring your pot to heat on the fire
  7. Add you oil into the pot and wait for it to bubble
  8. Add your Podalanka/Snake Gourd along with some water
  9. Add the turmeric powder into the pot and mix well
  10. Use a pot lid with a hole to let steam go away and wait for the water to evaporate nearly completely
  11. Add some more water to the mixture and add half the coconut milk
  12. Let it evaporate once more and finally pour in the rest of the coconut milk and your spice mix and mix it well
